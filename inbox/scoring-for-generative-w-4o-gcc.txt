Mid-to-late 2025 (Columns, Oct. 27 & Nov. 24): Messaging expands toward resilience and inclusivity, highlighting reopening efforts post-storms and celebrating the “diversity…which powers our economy.” Economic growth is now coupled with community strength and recovery. The Governor's language reflects a forward-looking, pro-growth tone while integrating economic development with workforce inclusion, environmental stewardship, and industry diversification.

What's happening with Medicaid in North Carolina?

The current status of Medicaid in North Carolina involves significant challenges and ongoing political and legal battles. Here are the key points:

Funding Issues: The state legislature has not yet passed a new budget to fully fund Medicaid for 3 million North Carolinians. Governor Josh Stein had sought to reduce Medicaid reimbursements to stretch funding, but courts have rejected that strategy​1​.

Reimbursement Rate Restorations: Governor Stein recently canceled Medicaid reimbursement rate reductions that were initiated two months ago. This decision was made to protect short-term access to care for vulnerable patients while political fights with Republican legislators continue​1​.

Court Rulings: Legal challenges to the reimbursement reductions have resulted in judicial rulings demanding that some rates return to pre-October levels. The state lost two recent court hearings over the validity of the cuts and faced other similar lawsuits​1​.

Political Pressure: Governor Stein has emphasized the need for the Republican-led General Assembly to fully fund the program. He stated that the program does not have enough money and that the courts have made it clear that the rates have to go back​1​.

Legislative Actions: The legislature had planned to convene next week, but any action or recorded votes are unlikely. Governor Stein has called for a special legislative session, but House Speaker Destin Hall and Senate leader Phil Berger refused to convene​1​.

Impact on Providers and Patients: The Medicaid shortfall continues, and the program could run out of money by March or April. This situation has led to significant stress and vulnerability for both providers and patients​1​.

Governor's Remarks: Governor Stein has consistently advocated for fully funding Medicaid as a necessary step to address healthcare gaps and improve safety outcomes for North Carolinians. He has linked Medicaid funding to broader public safety goals and emphasized the importance of investing in mental health and the healthcare workforce​2​.

What has the Governor said about public safety and law enforcement?

The Governor's messaging on public safety and law enforcement emphasizes a comprehensive approach that includes supporting law enforcement, addressing root causes of crime, and strengthening mental health services. Over time, the Governor has consistently highlighted the critical role of law enforcement while expanding the discussion to include broader systemic issues like mental health, addiction, and reentry support.

Key Themes and Direct Quotes:
Support for Law Enforcement:

The Governor has repeatedly acknowledged the sacrifices and challenges faced by law enforcement officers, emphasizing the need to support and retain them. In the State of the State address (2025-03-12), he stated, "We ask them to run towards danger when the rest of us might flee. Time and time again, they say yes – to protect and to serve." He also highlighted staffing challenges, proposing "salary increases for state law enforcement and correctional officers and signing bonuses for new officers" to address vacancies and ensure "more police officers on the beat."
In the October column (2025-10-27), he reiterated this commitment, proposing to "raise salaries for law enforcement officers so we can recruit and retain the best people for the job – people who are well-trained, community-focused, and not stretched dangerously thin."
Violence Prevention and Root Causes of Crime:

The Governor has consistently linked public safety to addressing root causes of crime. In the October column (2025-10-27), he introduced a $195 million public safety package, stating it "addresses the root causes of crime while also supporting our law enforcement and violence prevention systems." He emphasized that public safety "isn't just about arrests and patrols; it's about preventing harm before it happens."
He also highlighted investments in "violence prevention programs that keep kids out of gangs and help people battling addiction get healthy" (October column, 2025-10-27).
Mental Health as a Public Safety Priority:

The Governor has increasingly tied public safety to mental health, framing it as an essential component of crime prevention. In the October column (2025-10-27), he stated, "The connection between public safety and mental health is undeniable. If we invest in one, then we strengthen the other." He noted that "1.8 million adults live with a mental health condition" in North Carolina and stressed the importance of treatment and support.
He also called for strengthening mental health supports in the criminal justice system, including connecting individuals to treatment during their first encounters with law enforcement and ensuring proper care for incarcerated individuals (October column, 2025-10-27).
Gun Safety Measures:

While respecting Second Amendment rights, the Governor has advocated for safeguards to prevent access to firearms by those who pose a danger. In the October column (2025-10-27), he stated, "It puts safeguards in place to ensure those who are convicted felons, domestic abusers, or dangerously mentally ill don't have access to firearms."
Reentry Support for Formerly Incarcerated Individuals:

The Governor has emphasized the importance of supporting individuals reentering society after incarceration to reduce recidivism. In the October column (2025-10-27), he noted, "95% of people in prison will eventually return to cities and towns across North Carolina. If we close doors of opportunity for them, it's that much more likely that they'll return to the same choices that put them in jail in the first place."
Acknowledgment of Law Enforcement's Role:

The Governor frequently expresses gratitude for law enforcement's contributions. In the December column (2025-12-16), he stated, "I certainly have drawn hope from light – whether it's the teachers who educate our children, the law enforcement officers who keep us safe, or the everyday people who see neighbors in need and lend them a helping hand."
Similarly, in the November column (2025-11-24), he recognized "the law enforcement officers who put themselves in harm's way to protect our communities."

Early remarks, such as the State of the State address (2025-03-12), focused heavily on direct support for law enforcement, including salary increases and addressing staffing shortages.
By October 2025, the Governor's messaging had broadened to include systemic issues like mental health, addiction, and reentry support as integral to public safety (October column, 2025-10-27). This shift reflects a more holistic approach to addressing crime and safety.
Throughout, the Governor has maintained a consistent tone of respect and gratitude for law enforcement while advocating for proactive measures to prevent crime and improve community well-being.
In summary, the Governor's remarks on public safety and law enforcement underscore a balanced approach that combines support for law enforcement with investments in mental health, violence prevention, and reentry programs to build a safer and healthier North Carolina.
