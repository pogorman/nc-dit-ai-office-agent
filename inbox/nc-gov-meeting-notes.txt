AI Solution Demo for Governor's Communications Office: Patrick presented a comprehensive demo to Luke, William, Sofya, Jeremy, Anna, Jason, and I Hsieh, showcasing an AI-powered agent for the Governor's Communications Office that automates news monitoring, remarks search, and transcript proofreading, with technical details on architecture and deployment in Azure and Copilot Studio.05:21
.
Automated News Monitoring: Patrick explained the process of automating the identification of Governor Stein mentions in news articles, using an Azure Function to scrape governor.nc.gov daily, extract clean text with Mozilla Readability, embed articles with Azure OpenAI, and store them in Cosmos DB for semantic search. Staff can query these clips in Teams or M365 Copilot, receiving results ranked by meaning and keywords.

.
Remarks Search Functionality: The remarks search feature allows manual upload of speech files, which are processed by splitting into paragraphs, tagging with metadata (date, event, venue), embedding, and indexing in Cosmos DB. Staff can search for topics in Teams, and the AI synthesizes relevant passages with direct quotes and citations, following a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pattern.

.
Transcript Proofreading: Transcript proofreading is handled by GPT-40, which cleans up faulty transcripts from ASR or OCR, correcting errors and providing structured output with explanations for each change. The process is serverless, with no data stored, and estimated monthly costs are $120-200.

.
Power Platform Integration: Patrick described how Power Platform can be used for orchestration, such as sending daily digest emails, managing metadata in Dataverse, and triggering flows for remarks ingestion. Azure handles AI search and embedding, while Power Platform delivers the user experience and notifications.

.
Technical Architecture Overview: The solution architecture includes Azure OpenAI for embeddings, Cosmos DB for storage, Azure Functions for compute, API Management for routing and security, and Copilot Studio for agent orchestration. Managed identities and serverless design ensure security and scalability, with deployment to Teams and M365 Copilot.

Requirements Clarification and Expansion: Luke and Sofya clarified the Governor's office requirements, emphasizing the need for broader news monitoring beyond governor.nc.gov, including Bing search capabilities, and discussed potential future expansion to include video transcription and indexing for speeches.19:24
.
Scope of News Monitoring: Sofya and Luke raised questions about whether the news monitoring tool searches only official releases or the broader internet, noting the Governor's communications team wants to track mentions from all sources, not just their own site. Patrick confirmed the demo currently targets governor.nc.gov but is configurable to include Bing search for wider coverage.

.
Separation of Data Sources: Luke suggested separating databases or tools for governor.nc.gov and public news sources to clarify intended use, as the office needs both historical press release search and daily updates on external mentions.

.
Video Transcription and Indexing: Sofya proposed expanding the system to include YouTube videos and event recordings, asking about the feasibility of transcribing and vectorizing spoken content. Patrick acknowledged this as a valuable future enhancement and committed to investigating available cognitive services for transcription.

Model Selection and Copilot Studio Limitations: Luke, Patrick, and Jeremy discussed the limitations of Copilot Studio in GCC, which is restricted to GPT-4.0, and explored options for using newer models like GPT-5.2 via Azure AI Foundry, debating the impact on response quality and integration with Teams and M365 Copilot.26:29
.
GCC Model Restrictions: Luke expressed concern about being limited to GPT-4.0 in Copilot Studio for GCC, noting that newer models provide significantly better results, especially for dynamic chat use cases. Patrick confirmed that improvements are expected with future model releases but no firm date is available.

.
Azure AI Foundry Integration: Patrick explained that agents can leverage newer models deployed in Azure AI Foundry by routing queries through Azure Functions and APIs, which Copilot Studio can access via custom connectors, though the final response is still generated by GPT-4.0 in Copilot Studio.

.
Agent Deployment Options: Luke asked about deploying Foundry agents directly to Teams or M365 Copilot. Patrick and Jeremy clarified that, as of now, Copilot Studio is the main route for deployment, but they will investigate whether Foundry agents can be wrapped for Teams integration.

.
Impact of Model Choice on Output Quality: Luke emphasized that high-quality outputs require both good inputs and advanced models, preferring to use GPT-5.2 for response generation. Patrick and Jeremy agreed to escalate this need to the product team and explore workarounds.
